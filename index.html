A NOTE FROM THE CLERIC

Before you start flipping through this particular chronicle of magical malpractice and bureaucratic nonsense, my legal "representation"--which currently consists of a half-eaten sandwich and a stack of suspension notices--insists I lay down some ground rules.

The "It’s All Fake" Clause: This is a work of fiction. Any resemblance to actual persons, living, dead, or currently undergoing a messy "Sub-Resurrection Causal Inversion," is entirely coincidental. If you think a character looks like you, take it as a hint to work on your personality. The names, places, and incidents are products of an imagination currently fueled by adrenaline and spite.

The Geography of Grime: While this story takes place in a version of the modern world that looks suspiciously like the North Atlantic Magical Federation (shoutout to the damp, gray glory of Woodbridge, NJ), I’ve moved some landmarks around. If you go looking for a specific alley-clinic to get a bootleg entropy-scrub, don't. I’ve altered the street names to keep the Crimson-armored "phenomenal dunces" from knocking on the wrong doors.

The "Don't Be a Statistic" Warning: You’re going to see some Old Speech in here. You’re going to read about rites that displace reality like a quantum middle finger to the Standard Model. Do. Not. Attempt. Them. Seriously. If you try to recite an Aria in your bathtub and end up opening a portal to a dimension made of sentient static and bad vibes, that is on you. I am not responsible for accidental hauntings, localized heat death, or any supernatural entities that decide to squat in your living room without paying rent.

Intellectual Property & Rights: Every word, every scar, and every bit of "Some Ol' Bullshit" magic described in this pentalogy belongs to C.M. Roy. He owns the IP, the rights, and the moral high ground (even if the Academy disagrees). If you try to pirate this or reproduce it without permission, I can’t legally hex your Wi-Fi… but I’m a fourth-year Cleric with a lot of free time and a very specific set of skills. Don’t test the White Mage.

Now, stop reading the fine print and get to the story. I’ve got a booboos to tend to.

-- Bodhisunya "Bodhi" Rao
