Chapter 1: F*** Magic

Cracked linoleum slits my knees like spite. The kind of petty cut you don't notice until it's stinging through your trousers and the adrenaline backs off. My breath fogs against the kind of winter air that doesn't just invade but squats in corners like it's paying rent. This cheap community "alley"-clinic stinks of half-sterilized bandages, mana-rot, burnt hair, and the quiet panic that lingers when death overstays its welcome. Faintly, the sound of royalty-free "muzak" haunts its way through the air, the Infomercial on the waiting room TV turned up. The girl beneath me convulses again.

"Shh… shh, hey. You’re okay. You’re not—"

Her spine jerks, back arching like a snapped bowstring, mouth frothing in crescents of pink. Her ribs rattle like a bag of old dice under my hands. Blood where there shouldn't be blood, breath where there shouldn't be gaps. Every twitch is a clock tick closer to an obituary. She's losing air, time, and odds—all at once. And me? I'm gambling with Magic.

She’s maybe fifteen. Just a kid. Poor thing couldn't even get herself through the door. Pulse in freefall. Every rune-monitor in this slapdash trauma bay screams in a different dialect. A salvaged electrocardiogram kit whines next to a busted cauldron half-full of thaumic coolant. Someone has duct-taped a sanctified camphor talisman to the ceiling. Another has scrawled "LOVE IS A MIRACLE" over it in marker. Yeah? Try billing that to the regulator council.

I don't have time to guess how long she's been dying, or how poorly I set this place up, or why the universe keeps leaving corpses at my feet and asking for a receipt. She's here. I'm here. No one else is.

I press my palms to her sternum. Cold skin. Too light. The bones feel like they belong to someone who never finished growing.

"ThRun-kel Tuu Vaar'lenna"
No Echo. No Decay.

A pulse kicks from deep in my diaphragm as a Null Field snaps into existence. The air distorts, rippling outward like the heat shimmer on asphalt, then stills. Reality—mundane, lawful, sanitized physics—gets politely evicted. The field blooms. Spherical. Fragile. A dome of anti-reality laced with stolen spells and prayers I barely believe in. The "Old Speech" tends to have that kind of environmental impact, but I'm going for broke here. The standard Med Protocol Statis Field would not be able to handle the severity of her wounds, or the consequences if what I'm about to do is detected.

The tattoos etched across my forearms—entropy wards carved in ink and nerve endings—ignite. Pale violet. Huh. That's new. Questions for later.

"Shul'dara Vek Thae-lun"
Burn the end into Birth

I start the incantation. The real one. No Academy-template safezone spells. No healing-lite first-aid authorized by thaumaturgical ethics boards. This is real magic, raw and unlicensed, hates being bent into shape.

Each word tears its way up my throat like barbed wire. The null field hums louder, resisting the feed. Magic floods my limbs, burning as it threads itself through my arms. Through scar tissue from doing this sort of thing once-too-often, and spent muscle from just having a day-and-a-half. I force it down, into my wrists, into my fingers.

Her wound slowly start shuddering as they stitch themselves together, previously-spilled ichor tracing its way back inside it's host.

Not fast enough. Not nearly fast enough

She's drowning inside herself. Organ failure. Lungs filling. My field tenses. Feedback spirals along my spine. The limiter—every damn student has one, coded into our IDs, our aura signatures, even the cuffs of our coats—snaps back like a whip.

I push harder, audibly snarling. Focus. I redirect flow from my shoulders into the small fractures in my hands. The scars glow brighter –faint, ethereal cracks leaking sickly magenta light. My nails crack from the pressure.

"Come on," I mutter. "Come on, breathe."

Another pulse. Runes etched into the null field flare too wide, too sharp. If anyone nearby has a regulator beacon tuned, they’ll feel that. Screw it. I keep going.

"Vaelun"
"Please."

Not a spell. A plea.

Her alveoli begin to reknit. Capillaries reroute. Blood reverses direction like I'm unwinding her death.

Then—a single inhale. Wet, but real.

She jerks. Once. Then goes limp.

Still.

Still.

And then—

A breath.

Holy *s****.

She's alive.

For a single heartbeat, the world holds.

And then the null field screams.

Every rune-lamp in the alley explodes in sequence. Light. Shatter. Feedback. The regulator beacon embedded in the mana flow pings out like a flare. I’d bet half my tuition credits someone three blocks away just felt that. The spell collapses inward, folding the null field like paper into itself. Entropy backlash punches through my arms, singeing nerve endings raw. The air smells of ozone, blood, and divine sarcasm.

Sirens bloom on the horizon. Not the friendly kind.

I’m running out of time.

"Nihil sanctus. Resurgo."

The final phrase stings my gums as I seal the spell. I play it safe, ending the Triplet with Latin for stability, even if leaning on mass-production spellwork tastes a bit wrong in my mouth. Internal lattice reweaves. Circulatory rhythm stabilizes. Her color improves.

I wrap her in a silver-foil thermal, tucking the edges under her elbows. I reach into my coat, unhook the entropy-seal clasped to my wrist—my tracker, my confession, my crime—and place it beside her fingers.

Footsteps thud. Not fast. Intentional. Crimson-armored Regulators appear at the alley mouth in a staggered diamond formation. Wands already drawn. Suppression wards vibrating across their forearms.

Eight of them. Full complement. They were expecting worse.

"Hands where we can see them!"

I stay kneeling.

I raise my hands.

Magic still dances in my bones. My grip on it loosens as I let it go.

"I was just practicing my thesis," I say.

Helmet visors don’t crack smiles. Worth a shot.

"Step away from the girl."
"She’s stable. If you touch her before the field fully fades, she’ll spike and you’ll have killed her."

They don’t care. Two step forward.

"Uh-uh, fellas. Florence Nightingale's got that sweet chin music for you if you touch my patient".

Knowing their attention needs to be kept on me, I lean back on my heels and sit myself on my rear, my legs splayed and oh-so-conveniently blocking their path to the teen. The two give a glance at one another, before rejoining the group to form a half-circle. One begins the arrest ritual, voice flat, like I’m just another datapoint.

"Name. Student ID. Magical license class."

I tilt my head. Real slow. Let the silence drag an extra beat. Then I twist my lips into the most exaggerated, cartoonishly unimpressed pout I can muster—upper lip out, nose crinkled, eyelids half-mast like I’ve just smelled bureaucratic rot baking in the sun.

"No."

I really let that syllable drag out. Not shouted. Not whispered. Just... stated. Like gravity. Or spite. I swear even their hoods looked shocked.

"You refuse to comply?"
"I'm declining to comply, Chief"
I say, the vowels dripping with the sacred indignation of a Looney Tune on his last carrot.
"Subtle difference."

Somewhere in the back of my head, I hear a faint wahh-wahhhh fade in the distance. One of the Hoods with a Golden North Atlantic Magic Federation Insignia emblazoned on his right-arm gauntlet steps forward.

I wonder if he can see my smirk behind that visor.

A wand muzzle touches my temple. It hums.
Yeah. He saw it.

"Unauthorized miracle-working in a restricted zone."
"Emergency clause."
"No academy supervisor signed off."

That’s it. That’s the moment.

I throw my arms up in full-body disbelief, like I’m trying to scoop the sheer stupidity out of the air with my hands.

"She was dying! That's what makes it an emergency, you f***ing DUNCE!"

My hands are flying. I’m gesturing like a man explaining fire to cavemen. One hand clutches at my own hair while the other points furiously at the void where this specimen’s common sense should be. I'm stunned—stunned—that this conversation is real.

Then—

"And you are not cleared to make that call!"

He says it like it’s checkmate.

I let out a laugh that’s more exhale than joy. Stare at him like I just caught a goose trying to recite algebra.

"Oh, give your balls a tug!"

I sneer, but internally I feel a bit of my fight deflate, my hands dropping to their side with it. Of course not. I'm not a licensed miracle-worker. Just a student. Just a cleric-in-training with a chip on his shoulder and a naive deathgrip on his remaining conscience.

They slap the cuffs on. Cold metal laced with dampening sigils bites into the skin above my entropy scars. Numbness radiates. A sob tries to rise in my throat, but I’ve already learned how to kill those before they show.

How do lawyers not break down in "Frustration TearsTM" when they're arguing in court?? Goddamn Sociopaths.

I look down one last time. She’s breathing. God's above, she's actually still breathing. Sounds like a wet maraca, but it's there, and steady. Good. At least, way better than it was ten minutes ago.

I exhale. The cuffs hiss.

"F*** magic."

The shackles click shut.

Let's talk about magic.

"The supernatural and mysterious power of apparently influencing a course of events." The ultimate cop-out for writers the world over. "Spooky action at a distance" if you're Einstein-inclined.

Great. Those are the definitions you’d get if you asked a bored teenager who just binge-watched the entire Harry Potter franchise in one sitting. Magic, as mundanes tend to understand it, is basically a shortcut to doing stuff that would normally require effort, intelligence, or batteries. Which is hilarious, considering how much work it actually takes.

I refuse to bother with a "real definition" for concept so nebulous. It's a wonder anyone can even pretend to define it. You already know what Magic is. Or at least, you think you do. Fireballs. Lightning. Glowy runes. Whatever.
If it's shiny and not a mushroom, it's probably magic. How's that for a rigorous academic approach?

But what's really going on behind the curtain of sparkles and special effects? What is Magic?

I'll tell you.
Magic is Some Ol' Bulls***.

By and large, I'd say Harry Potter is to blame for people's preconceptions. Harry asks Hagrid why the magical world is secret and gets some trite spiel about how "ALL the muggles" would want to start using magic to solve all of their problems.

When the HELL has anything worked that way?

Everything has a cost. Especially magic. And that cost is rarely financial. Though in modern society, magic tends to follow the same principle everything else does: "If you're good at it, never do it for free." If you want to get something, you have to give something up. And with Magic, you never quite get or give what you expected to.

You know, while I'm on the topic, let's address the whole "muggle" and "mudblood" thing. NO ONE is investing time, effort, and energy on special slurs to call people based on magical lineage.
Well…some people might but there are weirdos like that everywhere, we don't consider outliers. Trust me, we already have more than enough slurs…

Case in point! My family is from India; I was raised as a citizen of the illustrious North Atlantic Magical Federation (a.k.a Woodbridge, New Jersey).

The Federation has had access to the internet and cell phones as long as it's Mundane Counterpart; someone called me the "n-word" in traffic on Thursday. The Magical and Mundane peoples are just as advanced (and just as backwards) as one another. Humans are the same no matter where you go.

We call that "progress".

The wagon stinks of ozone, piss, and bureaucratic boredom. Chain-runes hiss every time I shift, crackling in some ancient dialect designed to shame sorcerers before laws even existed. They line the bars like barbed wire made of light, looping lazy helixes across the metal mesh of the cage. Cold bleeds through the floor, right into my spine. Every time the wagon hits a bump, the chain-loop under my seat flares a glyph in warning—reminding me that one twitch too far will sear through the skin.

I sit cuffed, knees jammed against the slatted walls, ankles throbbing from where a dampening glyph kissed bone. The whole transport feels like an apology for compassion: cramped, loud, and rolling at a crawl down back alleys too narrow for emergency service but somehow wide enough to haul away unlicensed medics. The roof leaks condensation from spell-dampened air, and every drop that lands on me feels like the system spitting.

Two Regulators sit opposite me, crimson armor gleaming like sin in the pulse-light. Helmets full-face, visors opaque. They haven’t spoken since they shoved me in the cage. Probably trained not to. If they said anything, it might sound like doubt.

"You know,"
I say, loud enough to make their posture twitch,
"in some countries, healing without a license is called 'heroism.'"

No reply. Fine. I wasn’t really talking to them anyway.

"Bet the girl makes it,"
I add, tone too casual to be anything but pointed.
"Wouldn’t that be awkward? Alive and breathing and…not regulated, none of it."

The one on the left tilts their helmet a hair. A shift smaller than a nod, but I clock it. Progress. Or maybe checking their rune-feed for violations of the local noise ordnance.

"Just think,"
I continue, leaning back until the chain-runes tug sharp at my wrists,
"if I'd let her die, none of this paperwork would be necessary.
No reports.
No tribunal.
No sleepless nights wondering about all the regulations I broke
and if they're thinking about you too."

Still nothing. No verbal response, no twitch, no weight shift. But I catch the subtle tightening of a gauntlet, the telltale flutter of overclocked heart rate in the ambient hum of the suppression ward. The one on the right's pulse just spiked. Bingo.

"All those Combat Magic courses
they have you guys running must be
winding you up too tight,"
I say, furrowing my brow in a performance of concern.
"Must be hard, carrying all that armor and none of the moral weight. Heeeavy stuff, boys, heavy stuff."

That gets a reaction. Barely. The right one's shoulders twitch like he wants to shrug off the insult and can't decide if that counts as breaking formation.

"They must teach you all sorts of high-octane badassery, right? What is it these days? Arcane CQC? Wand-kata? Tactical Pilates? Wait wait, no I got it! Hot yoga!"

I lean forward with a grin I know will get under his skin.

"C'mon, big guy, what’s the secret sauce?
Do you guys chant in unison,
paint slogans on your chests before beating up street kids?
Or is it more of a spontaneous thing?"

The tension in the hothead's jaw is now a tectonic event. He hasn't moved, but the restraint in his posture is screaming. The kind of scream that comes right before a punch.

"Okay,"
I say, like I'm offering a lifeline.
"First answer that comes to mind:
One hundred of your elite unit versus one silverback gorilla—who wins?"

SLAM.

The sound is a thunderclap. The impact knocks the air out of me before I process the pain. My head cracks against the steel mesh wall with a dull thud. Stars burst behind my eyes. Tinnitus screams in both ears. Copper floods my mouth.

Hothead's up, gauntlet braced across my throat, the bulk of his armor pressing me into the van's reinforced wall like he's trying to meld me with it.

"KEEP. YOUR MOUTH. SHUT"

Each word is a hammer. Or maybe that was his fist. Each pause, a countdown. The kind of tone that tells you this isn't about me—not really. This is about something already cracked inside him and me showing him the fracture line.

My chains rattle as I raise my hands a few inches, chuckling painfully—not in surrender, just enough to show I'm not going to fight him. Not yet.

I lean my head back as far as the wall allows, staring up at the sigil-riddled ceiling.

"Not trying to provoke you,"
I croak, voice hoarse with pressure and pride.
"Well… not only. I'm just trying to fill the silence."

Before it fills me first, the last part I leave unsaid.

He holds there for a beat. Two. Breathing hard. The regulator's gauntlet trembles for half a second before he pushes off, steps back, and resumes his silent post like nothing happened. Like I imagined it. Like he's not one weak insult away from kicking my ribs in.

I cough, blood in my teeth now, and smile like I’m proud of it. Because I am.

Next checkpoint's close. I can feel it in the way the hum of the runes syncs with my heartbeat.

Good. I’m starting to run out of things to say.

The city outside blurs by. I try not to track it too closely. New Canaan's alleys all look the same when you're on your way to get publicly flogged by bureaucracy. But the moment we round a corner and the ward-glass clears, I get a clean look at the skyline. It hits like always: a punch to the throat made of glass and privilege.

Above the slums: the sky-bridges. Floating over half-frozen gutters and blown-out wards, they gleam like myth. Suspended by ley-tethers and taxpayer arrogance, the Academy towers connect overhead like the bones of some divine spinal cord. Clinics up there don’t use salvaged glyph-lamps or bootleg entropy seals. They have name-brand miracles. Branded. Billable. One floor for necrotics. Two for the elite-class trauma ward. Three for the insurance-backed rebirth suites.

A perfect place to get sick, if you can afford it.

Below, the street-level looks like a field hospital designed by a committee of vultures. Blood-slick slush slops under our wheels. Half the rune tags on the buildings are faded, overwritten with gang sigils or protest glyphs. I spot one that says "HEAL US OR HAUNT US" in spray-rune. Can't tell if it's a threat or a prayer. Someone tagged a ward-drain with a stencil of the Angel of Entropy, wings half-melted.

"Tell me,"
I ask the Regulators,
"when you signed up to protect the magical public interest,
was criminalizing CPR part of the recruitment poster?"

Stone silence. They’ve either muted their helm-audio or filed me under ‘rhetorical hazard.’

I shift again. Chain-runes bite my wrist like they resent my pulse. Probably do. These things were forged to bind deviance, not dialogue. Every move I make pings a silent report to the Academy’s Enforcement Matrix. I imagine some overworked functionary back at HQ scrolling through my vitals like a bored ex.

"Ever wonder what a miracle's worth?"
I murmur.
"Not in credits. In consequence."

Still nothing. Maybe they don't have clearance to talk about metaphysics. Maybe they just don't get paid enough.

We roll to a stop.

The gate checkpoint. Reinforced sigil-barrier, three layers deep, staffed by more crimson meat-suits holding arc-pikes like they were born gripping them. The wagon lurches as it docks into the intake platform. A dozen anti-teleport wards spark briefly as the runes verify we’re not smuggling eldritch contraband or curse-slugs. I sit very still, mostly to savor the irony. Once, this same checkpoint let me through with a lunchbox full of Class II healing runes and no questions asked.

The lead tech – some poor bastard who looks about fifteen under their helm – waves a wand through the window slit.

"Subject: Rao, Bodhi.

Suspected Class IV unauthorized miracle-working.

Confirmed thaumic residuals.

En route to Academy Custody."

One of the watchers glances at the readout. Her jaw tenses.

"Cleric track?"
"Fourth-Year,"
the other replies.
"S***."

The sound is barely audible, but I catch it. She covers with protocol.

"Proceed."

The wagon rolls on.

Through the next viewport I see a boy – maybe twelve – watching from behind a chain-fence, bundled in a coat five sizes too big. Her Brother I realize in recognition. I barely clocked the kid before, too preoccupied with saving the girl 's- his sister I remind myself -life. There is the faint recollection of him screaming bloody murder as I shunted him inside the clinic and set up my makeshift Trauma Bay in the alley. When our eyes meet, he makes the sign of healing. Not the Academy one. The old one. Fingers curled, thumb to sternum. Passed down by grandmothers who never made it past orientation exams. The kind of sign you learn when your miracles are all secondhand.

I give him a wink and a quick smile; the boy was worried to bits about his older sister, no need to stress him out any further.

The cage jolts as we pass under another suppression arch. Somewhere behind me, an etheric scanner starts logging my aura decay. It’ll show entropy scars flaring on contact with the girl. It won’t log the hesitation in my hands. The silent prayer. The look in her brother's face when she finally stopped seizing. The breath that wasn’t supposed to come back.

None of that goes in the file.

"You know what your problem is?"
I say to the rune-bars.

They don’t answer.

"You're efficient.
You're clean.
You're quiet.
You erase stories as well as scars."

Still nothing.

But the runes glow a little dimmer. Or maybe that's just me.

Next stop: Containment Ward 7-C. Cold fire, automated scanners, and a medic who thinks I’m a contaminated tool.

Can’t wait.
